Fairs & Festivals - The Life of Gujarat

Rann Utsav

Rann Utsav is a vibrant cultural festival celebrated annually in the Kutch district of Gujarat, India. This spectacular event takes place in the white salt desert of the Great Rann of Kutch, a unique and vast expanse that turns into a surreal landscape under the moonlight.

Location: Kutch

International Kite Festival

The International Kite Festival (Uttarayan) in Gujarat is one of the most vibrant and eagerly anticipated events in India. Celebrated primarily in Ahmedabad and across the state of Gujarat, this festival draws kite enthusiasts from around the world to participate in a spectacular display of color, creativity, and skill in the skies.


Location: Ahmedabad and other location of Gujarat

Navratri

Navaratri is one of the most vibrant and widely celebrated festivals in Gujarat, known for its grandeur, devotion, and especially for the traditional dance forms of Garba and Dandiya Raas. The festival, which spans nine nights and ten days, honors the divine feminine energy, known as Shakti, and celebrates the victory of good over evil.



Location: All around Gujarat

Janmashtami

Janmashtami, also known as Krishna Janmashtami or Gokulashtami, is one of the most significant festivals in Gujarat, celebrated with great devotion and enthusiasm to mark the birth of Lord Krishna, the eighth incarnation of Lord Vishnu. The festival usually falls in August and is observed on the eighth day (Ashtami) of the Krishna Paksha (dark fortnight) in the Hindu month of Shravana.

Location: Dwarka & Dakor Lord Krishna temples

Tarnetar Fair

The Tarnetar Fair is a renowned annual festival held in the Tarnetar village of Gujarat, India. Known for its vibrant celebrations and cultural significance, this fair is one of the most prominent tribal festivals in the state, drawing visitors from across India and abroad. The fair typically takes place in late September or early October, coinciding with the Hindu festival of Navaratri.



Location: Thangadh, Surendranagar

Rath Yatra

The Rathyatra in Gujarat is one of the most celebrated and iconic festivals in the state, marked by grand processions, religious fervor, and cultural festivities. The term "Rathyatra" refers to a chariot festival where deities are paraded through the streets in massive, elaborately decorated chariots. This festival holds immense significance in Gujarat and is celebrated with great enthusiasm, particularly in the city of Ahmedabad.


Location: Ahmedabad
